Rural and countryside

Organisations:

Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs, Environment Agency and Natural England

Related policies:

This page may not be up to date

These policies relate to the Coalition Government 2010-15. Following the general election of 6 May 2015, this page will be updated in due course.

Rural economy 3

Economic growth is a top priority. We want rural areas to contribute to and benefit from this growth, even though they face particular challenges.

  1. Rural Development Programme for England

    The Rural Development Programme for England is part of the Common Agricultural Policy and is jointly funded by the EU. We plan to spend £3.5 billion from 2014-2020 to help improve the environment, support business and promote growth in local economies.

    • Funding: £3.5 billion
    • Started: Jan 2013
    • Led by: DEFRA

  2. Skills and knowledge transfer programme

    We’re organising a range of training and networking programmes for business people in rural areas.

    • Funding: £20 million
    • Started: March 2012
    • Led by: DEFRA

  3. Rural growth network pilots

    We’ve set up 5 pilot rural growth networks (RGNs) designed to reduce barriers to economic growth in the countryside. We’re expecting to create up to 3,000 jobs and 700 businesses over the 5 pilot RGN areas.

    • Funding: £2.1 million
    • Started: Jan 2013
    • Led by: DEFRA

Access to the countryside 3

Everybody should have the opportunity to access, use and enjoy England’s natural environment and outdoor spaces. By protecting and improving access, we can help more people enjoy the countryside.

  1. Funding the Canal & River Trust

    We’re funding the Canal & River Trust (CRT), the charity which was set up in July 2012 to manage many of the UK’s inland waterways, taking over responsibility from British Waterways.

    • Funding: £800 million over 15 years
    • Started: July 2012
    • Led by: DEFRA

  2. Simplifying the processes for recording and making changes to public rights of way

    We committed to consulting on simplifying the processes for recording and making changes to public rights of way, and ran the consultation in 2013.

    • Funding: n/a
    • Started: July 2012
    • Led by: DEFRA

  3. Enabling direct elections to National Park authorities

    We’re reviewing the governance of National Parks and the Broads to improve local accountability. Draft laws will be published, when Parliamentary time allows, to enable direct elections to National Park authorities in England.

    • Funding: n/a
    • Started: July 2012
    • Led by: DEFRA

Including rural voices 5

Government has not always been successful in considering the needs of rural communities when making policy. We have to make sure all our policies are ‘rural proofed’: this means we take into account the needs of our rural communities and make sure they are treated fairly.

  1. Free vote on hunting with dogs

    The Coalition agreement included a promise for a free vote in the House of Commons on the repeal of the Hunting Act 2004. If the vote is in favour of repeal, we will introduce a repeal bill in both Houses of Parliament in due course.

    • Funding: n/a
    • Started: n/a
    • Led by: DEFRA

  2. Rural and Farming Network

    In 2011, we set up the Rural and Farming Network (RFN). This is a network of 17 groups across rural England that keep government informed of rural communities’ concerns.

    • Funding: ?
    • Started: 2011
    • Led by: DEFRA

  3. Rural Community Action Network

    Defra funds the Rural Community Action Network (RCAN). This is a network of 38 charitable local development agencies across England. RCAN keeps Defra current with local knowledge from rural areas.

    • Funding: ?
    • Started: ?
    • Led by: DEFRA

  4. Rural proofing

    We want all government policies and programmes to work as well for rural communities as they do for people living in towns. This is called ‘rural proofing'. We've published guidelines for government on achieving this.

    • Funding: n/a
    • Started: ?
    • Led by: DEFRA

  5. Rural statement

    We published our rural statement in September 2012. It confirms our commitment to rural communities and sets out our support for rural areas.

    • Funding: n/a
    • Started: ?
    • Ended: ?
    • Led by: DEFRA
